# ARLO — SEO Strategy & Per-Page Playbook

_Source of truth for how we rank ARLO. Built from live SE Ranking data (US database, pulled 2026-07). Update the numbers quarterly — search volume for this category is moving fast._

---

## The core insight (read this first)

Most SEO playbooks (including the "Claude Code SEO masterclass") are built for an **established local-services market**: pick `service + city`, filter to difficulty ≤ 30 / volume ≥ 100, and there's demand waiting. ARLO breaks that model in two ways, and the live data proves it:

1. **Our category has almost no search volume yet.** `search console in claude`, `mcp for agencies`, `claude for seo`, `ai client reporting` all return **no data** — nobody searches them today. But `mcp server` grew **270 → 49,500** searches in 12 months. The category is real and exploding; it just hasn't reached our exact phrasings.
2. **The demand our buyers already have lives in the "reporting / tool" family** — and it's full of low-difficulty gems: `ppc reporting tool` (KD 11), `client reporting` (KD 6), `automate seo reporting` (KD 14), `pay per click reporting` (KD 7).

### The rule this produces: every page gets a **dual anchor**

| Anchor | Job | Example (Google Ads page) |
|---|---|---|
| **Money keyword** | Traffic *today*. An established, low-KD reporting term. Goes in the title, H1 area, first 100 words. | `ppc reporting tool` |
| **Category keyword** | Traffic *tomorrow*. The ARLO-native "Claude / MCP" phrasing. Near-zero competition, so we rank #1 trivially and own it as the category grows. | `google ads reporting in claude` |

We do **not** bet the pages on the category terms yet — they don't drive traffic. We *own them cheaply* while ranking for money keywords that convert now.

---

## Keyword difficulty caveat

- **Difficulty (KD)** and **volume** are from SE Ranking, US database. Volumes for several "tool" terms show a large current-month spike over a flat 12-month baseline — treat the **12-month baseline** as the truer number and re-pull before making big bets.
- Prioritize **KD ≤ 25** for near-term wins on a young domain. KD 25–45 is a 6–12 month play backed by internal links + blog. KD > 60 (e.g. `claude mcp` 70, `mcp server` 88) is a long-game / blog-only target — do not put it on a conversion page's title.

---

## Priority order (where to spend effort first)

1. **Google Ads Specialist** — strongest, fastest. A whole cluster at KD 7–17 with real volume. Rank this first.
2. **Account Manager** — `client reporting` cluster, KD 6–22. Also a secondary hub for the reporting theme.
3. **SEO Specialist** — `automate seo reporting` (KD 14) + a deep 480–590-vol cluster.
4. **Home** — category hub; carries brand + owns the `mcp for agencies` category terms.
5. **Agency Owner** — moderate; broader/mixed-intent terms.
6. **Solo Business Owner** — informational-leaning, harder SERP; support with blog.
7. **Meta / Paid Social** — weakest *search* demand (mostly no data) + partly Phase-2. Treat as a positioning/conversion page, not a traffic play.

# Per-page briefs

## 1. Home — `/arlo`  ·  role: category HUB

The highest-authority page. It carries the brand and is the only page strong enough to chase the hard category head-term over time. Its main SEO job is **internal-link distribution**, not ranking for a money keyword.

- **Primary (category, own it now):** `mcp for agencies`, `marketing mcp`, `marketing mcp server` — ~0 volume today, ~0 competition. Rank #1 by default; harvest as the category grows.
- **Primary (long-game head term):** `claude mcp` (Vol 4,400 · KD 70) — blog-supported, 12-month horizon. Do **not** over-optimize the homepage title for it.
- **Money angle:** `ai marketing reporting`, `marketing reporting automation` (Vol 50 · KD 21).
- **Cluster:** `claude for marketing agencies`, `connect claude to google analytics / ads / search console`, `ai client reporting`, `mcp marketing`.
- **Title (recommended):** `ARLO — The MCP Layer for Agencies | Ask Claude About Any Client`
- **First-100-words:** must contain "MCP" + "agencies" + "Claude" + "reporting" (it mostly does).
- **Links OUT:** to all 6 service pages (role anchor text: "for SEO specialists", "for Google Ads managers"…), to `/destinations`, to the blog hub, to `/pricing`.
- **Links IN:** every service page, every blog post, breadcrumb root.

# Internal linking architecture (hub-and-spoke)

```
                         ┌──────────────┐
                         │   HOME /arlo │  ← every page links up here
                         │ (category hub)│  → links down to all 6 roles + blog
                         └──────┬───────┘
              ┌─────────────────┼──────────────────┐
        ┌─────▼─────┐    ┌──────▼──────┐     ┌──────▼──────┐
        │ Agency    │    │  Account    │     │   Solo      │
        │ Owner     │───▶│  Manager    │◀────│  Owner      │
        │(funnel top)│   │(reporting hub)│   └─────────────┘
        └─────┬─────┘    └──┬───┬───┬───┘
              │             │   │   │
        ┌─────▼──────┐ ┌────▼─┐ │ ┌─▼──────┐
        │ (links to  │ │ SEO  │ │ │ Meta / │
        │ all roles) │ │ Spec.│◀┼▶│ Paid   │
        └────────────┘ └───┬──┘ │ │ Social │
                           │ ┌──▼──────┐ └────────┘
                           └▶│ Google  │
                             │ Ads ⭐  │
                             └─────────┘
     Blog posts (future) ─────▶ link UP to the matching money page
                                with exact/partial-match anchor text
```

Rules:
- **Every page links to Home; Home links to every page.** (Breadcrumb schema already does part of this.)
- **Specialist pages interlink as siblings** and link **up to Account Manager**.
- **Agency Owner links down** to all six roles (it's the top of the buyer funnel).
- **Account Manager is the secondary hub** for the reporting cluster.
- **Blog posts pass equity UP** to their money page with keyword-rich anchors (e.g. a "how to automate PPC reporting" post links to the Google Ads page with anchor `ppc reporting tool`).
- Aim for **3–5 internal links** and **2–3 external authoritative links** per page (the current pages under-link — this is the #1 on-page gap).

# The second engine: blog-at-scale (not built yet)

Service pages capture the money keywords. **Blog posts build the topical authority that lifts every service page** (the "rising tide"). Recommended first clusters, each post linking UP to its money page:

- **PPC cluster → Google Ads page:** "How to automate PPC reporting", "PPC report template (and why templates break)", "MCC reporting without switching accounts".
- **SEO cluster → SEO page:** "How to connect Search Console to Claude", "Automate your monthly SEO report", "Indexation audits in one prompt".
- **Client-reporting cluster → Account Manager page:** "Client reporting that writes itself", "Answer the 4pm client ping in 60 seconds".
- **Category cluster → Home:** "What is MCP (for agencies)?", "MCP vs. Looker Studio dashboards", "Connect Claude to your marketing stack".

Cadence: ramp slowly (1–2/week, not 50 at once) to avoid volume-spike penalties. Bottle the workflow into a reusable Claude skill once the template is proven.

# Do / Don't (carried from the masterclass, filtered for a SaaS category-creator)

- **Do** chase the KD 7–20 reporting gems first — they convert now. **Don't** build conversion-page titles around KD 70+ category head-terms.
- **Do** own the zero-volume category phrasings cheaply (`X in claude`). **Don't** expect them to drive traffic this year.
- **Do** front-load the money keyword in title + first 100 words. **Don't** keep the brand-first "ARLO for X" title on pages we want to rank.
- **Do** interlink hub-and-spoke and add external authority links. **Don't** ship isolated pages (current gap).
- **Do** verify Lighthouse ≥ 95 with the shader running. **Don't** let a beautiful WebGL hero tank Core Web Vitals.
- **Don't** buy backlinks / PBNs. Earn them (guest posts, HARO/journalist queries, broken-link swaps, product launches on real publications).
