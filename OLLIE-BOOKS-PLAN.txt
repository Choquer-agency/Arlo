1. WHAT OLLIE BOOKS IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ollie Books is an MCP (Model Context Protocol) connector that plugs Claude
directly into QuickBooks Online. Connect once, then ask questions in plain
English and get live answers from your real books:

  "What was my net income last quarter, month by month?"
  "Which customers have overdue invoices, and how much do they owe?"
  "How do my expenses this year compare to last year — what grew the most?"
  "Give me a Monday-morning pulse on the business."

No dashboards to build. No exports. No stale reports. The data is fetched live
from QuickBooks at the moment of the question, returned to the user's own AI
session, and never stored. (Pass-through architecture — proven in ARLO.)

ONE-LINER: Ollie Books turns QuickBooks into a conversation.

THE WEDGE / CORE INSIGHT
QuickBooks holds the single most valuable dataset a small business owns — and
almost no owner can get answers out of it without clicking through reports they
don't understand or paying a bookkeeper to interpret them. Meanwhile, millions
of those same owners now use Claude/ChatGPT daily. Ollie Books is the missing
wire between the two. The product IS the prompt.

2. WHO IT'S FOR (ICPs, in priority order)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ICP 1 — SMALL BUSINESS OWNERS (primary; the emotional core of the brand)
  • 1–20 employees, on QuickBooks Online, does their own light bookkeeping or
    has an outsourced bookkeeper they see once a month
  • Pain: "I have QuickBooks but I don't actually KNOW how my business is
    doing." Reports feel like accountant language. Decisions get made on vibes.
  • Dream outcome: a pulse on the business, on demand, in their own words
  • Where they live: r/smallbusiness, r/Entrepreneur, Facebook groups,
    YouTube "QuickBooks tutorial" land, local chambers, X/LinkedIn SMB content

ICP 2 — ACCOUNTANTS & BOOKKEEPERS (the multiplier; mirrors ARLO's agency model)
  • Firms and solo ProAdvisors managing 10–200 client QBO files
  • Pain: every client call starts with 30 minutes of report-pulling; clients
    ask "how am I doing?" constantly and answering doesn't scale
  • Dream outcome: answer any client question in one sentence; proactive
    advisory ("3 of your clients have AR blowing up") instead of rear-view
    compliance work. Advisory services are the industry's stated growth path —
    Ollie Books is an advisory-services engine.
  • Where they live: Intuit ProAdvisor community, r/Bookkeeping, Bookkeeping
    Side Hustle FB group, accounting Twitter/X, Karbon/Canopy content orbit

ICP 3 — MEDIUM BUSINESSES (10–100 employees; finance lead or fractional CFO)
  • Pain: the CEO asks finance for numbers; finance builds decks; three days
    pass. Fractional CFOs juggle several companies' books.
  • Dream outcome: self-serve answers for the CEO; the fractional CFO walks
    into every meeting already briefed
  • Note: this ICP pulls the roadmap toward budgets vs. actuals, cash-flow
    forecasting, and class/location dimensions. Serve after 1 & 2.

4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE (and the honest answer to each)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Intuit Assist (built into QBO)     — Generic, shallow, locked inside
    Intuit's UI. Ollie's answer: we live where the user already thinks (Claude),
    with real analytical depth and cross-question follow-ups.
  • LiveFlow / G-Accon / Sheets syncs  — Spreadsheet pipelines; you still build
    the model. Ollie's answer: no destination, no template — just the answer.
  • Fathom / Syft / Jirav (BI layer)   — Beautiful reports for accountants,
    $50–$150+/mo, weeks of setup, made for presenting not asking. Ollie's
    answer: conversation beats dashboard for 90% of daily questions.
  • "Just upload a CSV to ChatGPT"     — Manual, stale, error-prone, and a
    genuine data-hygiene risk. Ollie's answer: live + scoped + never stored.
  • DIY MCP builders                   — Developers can wire this themselves.
    Ollie's answer: OAuth, token refresh, multi-company, security, and support
    in 5 minutes for non-developers. (Same dynamic ARLO faces; it's fine.)

MOAT DIRECTION: speed to the accountant channel + multi-client management +
trust brand. Individual features will be copied; the distribution wedge and
"the safe one" reputation compound.

5. PRICING STRATEGY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LAUNCH (months 0–3): FREE while in early access — same play as ARLO.
  No credit card, no limits, no lock-in language ("choose a plan or walk away
  when pricing lands; nothing auto-charged"). Goal: installs, testimonials,
  and a hungry feedback loop.

TARGET MODEL (validate against usage data before shipping):
  • Owner        $29/mo   — 1 company, unlimited questions
  • Firm         $99/mo   — 10 client companies, team seats, per-client scoping
  • Firm+        $249/mo  — 50 companies, audit log, priority support
  Anchor: one hour of bookkeeper time costs more than a month of Ollie.

CHANNEL ECONOMICS: Intuit App Store listing takes a rev share but delivers the
highest-intent traffic that exists for this product. Treat it as CAC, not tax.

10. RISKS & MITIGATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Intuit platform risk (API terms, rate limits, Assist competing):
    → stay squeaky-clean on data handling; App Store partnership deepens the
      relationship; multi-ledger future (Xero, FreshBooks) removes single-
      vendor dependence — name it on the roadmap, build it later.
  • Trust objection on financial data:
    → pass-through architecture front and center; security page day one;
      SOC2 conversation when revenue justifies it.
  • Accuracy expectations ("the AI said my profit was X"):
    → answers cite the report + date range they came from; encourage
      verification framing in docs; never editorialize numbers.
  • Verification/review queues (Intuit):
    → start reviews week one; they gate the best channel.
